Notes that polish themselves

You snap. It writes.

Take the photo and walk away. Musing reads it — the merchant and total on the receipt. The brand and model on the appliance. The workout scrawled on the gym whiteboard, every rep. It writes the note, and it knows what kind of thing it is. Nothing to type. Nothing to file.

Your notes, gathered

Tap one. See them all.

Every note wears a tag for what it is. Tap it, and Musing gathers every other one like it — every receipt, every workout, every bottle you liked. Open search and your notebook lays out its own map: each kind you keep, and how many. A collection that builds itself, never a folder you keep.

Magical search

Just ask.

No folders. No tags. No filenames — Musing never asked you for any. Say it the way you'd say it out loud: “my receipts,” “workouts last month,” “the Home Depot receipt.” Musing understands what kind of thing you mean and when it happened, and tells you why each note matched.

Ask for a kind or a time and it lands before the keyboard settles — no spinner, no signal needed, no credit spent.
