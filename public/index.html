An object, not an interface

It reads like paper.

There are no tab bars in Musing, and no back buttons. Every screen rises as a page above your notebook; pull down anywhere and it falls away, all the way home. Drag to leaf through your notes. Swipe up and the notebook spreads into a timeline of everything you've kept.

Quietly intelligent

Notes that finish themselves.

Type a fragment, speak it, or snap a photo — Musing cleans it up, reads the image, and can research the note on the live web, sources included. Write “remind me…” and a real Apple Reminder appears. You jot half a thought; Musing hands back something finished.

Find anything by asking.

No folders. No tags. Search the way you'd ask a friend — “that book Sarah recommended,” “the rainy café idea” — and Musing surfaces the note you mean, not just the words you typed. Every note quietly remembers where and when it happened, so a half-remembered moment stays findable.

Two moods

Stone by day.
Espresso by night.

Warm stone light, deep espresso dark — typography set in Spectral and Newsreader, made to be read. Every detail of Musing was drawn before it was coded.
